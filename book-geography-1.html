Why This Matters

This prototype asks a narrow question: when a geography textbook describes China, regions, peoples, and administrative units, does it use the modern term 民族? If the word appears rarely or not at all, that absence is also meaningful. It may suggest that the textbook organized difference through older categories such as region, place, tribe, race, or administration, rather than through the later vocabulary of nationality or ethnicity.
