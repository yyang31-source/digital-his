Finding from the Subject Comparison

In the 1930s, when Chinese nationalism intensified under the pressure of Japanese aggression, these five subjects helped cultivate students' national consciousness from different directions. The frequency differences should therefore be read as differences in curricular emphasis: some subjects named 姘戞棌 more directly, while others taught national belonging through their own forms of school knowledge.

The KWIC examples make those differences clearer. With context, 姘戞棌 shifts from historical struggle to civic population and survival, from territorial knowledge to literary feeling, and from family order to moral responsibility. The same keyword taught national consciousness differently depending on the subject in which students encountered it.
